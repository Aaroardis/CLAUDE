## SCRIPT 02 — "The Biggest Lie About Monogamy"
**Status:** `DRAFT`
**Length:** ~25 seconds | **Hook Type:** Controversial opener (stops the scroll)

The biggest lie men were told about monogamy?
That it's natural — and that if you're struggling with it, something is wrong with you.

Humans have practiced non-monogamy in some form for most of recorded history.
The idea that *one woman should meet every single one of your needs* forever?
That's modern. That's recent. And it's putting enormous pressure on you both.

Polyamory isn't the answer for everyone.
But understanding it will change how you see all relationships.

## SCRIPT 03 — "Three Things Polyamory Actually Requires"
**Status:** `DRAFT`
**Length:** ~30 seconds | **Hook Type:** List / Curiosity

Men think polyamory is about…
more sex. More options. Having your cake and eating it too.

Here's what it *actually* requires:

One — radical honesty. Even when it's uncomfortable.
Two — the ability to sit with jealousy and get *curious* about it instead of reactive.
Three — a genuine commitment to your partner's emotional experience — not just your own desires.

Polyamory is one of the most demanding personal development paths I know.
And when it's done right? It's one of the most beautiful.

## SCRIPT 05 — "What Jealousy Is Actually Telling You"
**Status:** `DRAFT`
**Length:** ~35 seconds | **Hook Type:** Reframe / Insight

If you're exploring open relationships and jealousy hits — don't panic.
Jealousy doesn't mean you love her too much.
It doesn't mean polyamory is wrong for you.

Jealousy is a signal.
Usually pointing to one of three things:

A fear that you're not enough.
A belief that love is scarce — that if she loves someone else, there's less for you.
Or an unmet need you haven't named yet.

When you get *curious* about jealousy instead of reactive…
it stops running your relationships.

That's the work. And I'm here for it with you.
