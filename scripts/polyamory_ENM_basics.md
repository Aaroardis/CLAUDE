## SCRIPT 01 — "Nobody Taught Us This"
**Length:** ~20 seconds | **Hook Type:** Relatable truth

Nobody taught us how to be in a relationship.
We got fairy tales. We got wedding movies.
We got "find the one" — and then silence.

No one told us that love is a *skill*.
That jealousy is information, not a verdict.
That wanting more than one person doesn't make you broken.

That's what I'm here for.
Follow if you want real tools for real love.

## SCRIPT 02 — "The Biggest Lie About Monogamy"
**Length:** ~25 seconds | **Hook Type:** Controversial opener (stops the scroll)

The biggest lie we were told about monogamy?
That it's natural.

Humans have been practicing non-monogamy — in some form — for most of recorded history.
The idea that *one person should meet every single one of your needs* forever?
That's modern. That's recent. And honestly?
It's putting a *lot* of pressure on love.

Polyamory isn't the answer for everyone.
But understanding it will change how you see all relationships.

## SCRIPT 03 — "Three Things Polyamory Actually Requires"
**Length:** ~30 seconds | **Hook Type:** List / Curiosity

People think polyamory is about…
having more sex.
Having more options.
Having your cake and eating it too.

Here's what it *actually* requires:

One — radical honesty. Even when it's uncomfortable.
Two — the ability to sit with jealousy and get *curious* about it instead of reactive.
Three — a genuine commitment to other people's emotional experience.

Polyamory is one of the most demanding personal development paths I know.
And when it's done well? It's one of the most beautiful.

## SCRIPT 04 — "She's Not 'Too Much'"
**Length:** ~15 seconds | **Hook Type:** Short statement — high shareability

If a woman is "too much" for you…
she's not too much.

She's just too much *for where you currently are.*

That's not an insult. That's an invitation.

## SCRIPT 05 — "What Jealousy Is Actually Telling You"
**Length:** ~35 seconds | **Hook Type:** Reframe / Insight

Jealousy doesn't mean you love someone too much.
It doesn't mean polyamory is wrong for you.
And it doesn't mean your partner did something wrong.

Jealousy is a signal.
Usually? It's pointing to one of three things:

A fear that you're not enough.
A belief that love is scarce — that if they love someone else, there's less for you.
Or an unmet need that hasn't been named yet.

When you get *curious* about jealousy instead of reactive…
it stops running your relationships.

That's the work. And I'm here for it with you.

## SCRIPT 07 — "The Village Model"
**Length:** ~25 seconds | **Hook Type:** Reframe / Ancient wisdom

Humans weren't designed to raise children, build community, and meet each other's every emotional and erotic need — with *one* person.

That's not romance. That's a staffing crisis.

Our ancestors lived in villages.
Love was distributed. Care was distributed. Presence was distributed.

Polyamory isn't a new trend.
It's a return.

There's a word for the joy you feel when someone you love is loved by someone else.

It's called *compersion.*

And yes — it's real.
No — it's not easy at first.
But learning to feel it?

It'll change everything you think you know about love.

## SCRIPT 10 — "What Ethical Non-Monogamy Is Not"
**Length:** ~30 seconds | **Hook Type:** Myth-busting — drives comments/debate

Let me clear something up.

Ethical non-monogamy is not an excuse to avoid commitment.
It's not a phase. It's not about fear of intimacy.
And it is absolutely *not* cheating with extra steps.

ENM requires more communication, more self-awareness, and more emotional skill than most monogamous relationships ever ask for.

The "ethical" part is the whole point.

---

## SCRIPT 11 — "The Question Nobody Asks Before Getting Into a Relationship"
**Length:** ~25 seconds | **Hook Type:** Curiosity gap

Before most people get into a relationship, they ask:
Are we attracted to each other?
Do we have fun together?
Do we want the same things in life?

What almost nobody asks is:
*What do we each believe love is actually for?*

Is it for security? For growth? For pleasure? For legacy?

That one question will tell you more about compatibility
than every date you've ever been on.

---

## SCRIPT 12 — "Why I Work With People in Open Relationships"
**Length:** ~35 seconds | **Hook Type:** Personal authority / POV

People sometimes ask me why I specialize in polyamory and ENM.

Here's my honest answer:

The people doing this work are some of the most *self-aware* humans I've ever met.
They've had to examine their attachment wounds.
They've had to learn to communicate needs that most people never name.
They've had to sit in discomfort and *grow* from it.

They chose love — consciously. On purpose. Every day.

And that's exactly the kind of love I believe we're all moving toward.
Whether we call it polyamory or not.
