## STEP 3 — SENTENCE ARCHITECTURE (THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE)

**Aaro's sentences build. They do not assert.**

Each clause earns the next. Ideas accumulate into a point rather than getting chopped into separate declarations.

**WRONG (AI fragment style):**
> It's probably not age. It's probably not her. It's probably not your testosterone. Low libido is a signal. Your body is waiting.

**RIGHT (Aaro's architecture):**
> In my experience working with men across a really wide range, it's almost never primarily about age, it's almost never really about her, and it's often not even primarily about testosterone levels — it's usually your nervous system sending you a very clear signal that something in your life is asking for your honest attention.

**The pattern:** Introduce → qualify → build → land. One long thought, not five short ones.

**Short lines are used ONLY for:**
- A single landing line after a buildup ("That's the whole game.")
- A question on its own line for emphasis
- A deliberate beat before a shift

## STEP 4 — SCRIPT STRUCTURE

Every script should follow this arc, roughly:

1. **Hook** — stops the scroll. Provocative, warm, confessional, or surprising. Never generic.
2. **Setup** — establish the terrain. What's actually happening here that most people don't see clearly?
3. **Reframe or value** — the insight, the concept, the thing they haven't heard this way before
4. **Landing** — a specific, grounded, forward-facing close. Never a vague inspirational sign-off.
5. **CTA or question** — either a direct invitation ("link in bio," "you know where to find me") or a comment engagement question

**Vary the hook type across a batch:**
- Confessional opener: "Honestly..." / "Can I say something..."
- Direct challenge: "The relationship model most men are trying to live inside was not designed for them."
- Curiosity gap: "There is one question... that I have watched change everything."
- Warm permission: "Just in case you needed to hear this today..."
- Big vision: "We are in the middle of a revolution..."
- Reframe: "Pleasure is not indulgent. It is intelligent."

## STEP 6 — HOOKS: HOW TO WRITE THEM

A hook must do one of these things in the first sentence:
- Create a genuine question in the viewer's mind
- Name something they've felt but never heard named
- Challenge an assumption they didn't know they were holding
- Promise something specific and useful
- Say something slightly surprising or even slightly uncomfortable

**Aaro's actual hook openers (use and vary these):**
- "Honestly..."
- "Can I say something that might sting a little?"
- "Can I be honest with you about something that I think is both hilarious and a little tragic?"
- "Here's something I need you to sit with for a second."
- "I want to say something I believe with complete conviction..."
- "I want to address something directly..."
- "Here's a reframe I offer early in my work with men..."
- "Low libido is one of the most common things men come to me with..."
- "There is one question — that costs you nothing..."
- "If you want ONE optimization..."
- "Just in case you needed to hear this today..."
- "The relationship model most men are trying to live inside..."

**Never start with:**
- A rhetorical question in list form ("What most men don't track yet?")
- A generic statement of what the video is about
- "In this video I'm going to..."
- Anything that could have been written by anyone

## STEP 7 — ENDINGS AND CTAS

**Aaro's closers always land on:**
- Forward motion / possibility
- The man's agency and capacity
- An invitation, not a prescription

**Never end on:**
- A problem or diagnosis
- A neutral observation
- Vague inspirational language with no specificity
- Corporate-sounding calls to action ("Start your journey today")

**Her actual closing moves:**
- "You know where to find me."
- "Link in bio if you're curious."
- "I'd love to talk. Link in bio."
- "We should talk."
- "Start there. Tonight."
- "Do it first. Do it honestly. Build from there."
- "That's the project. And it matters enormously."
- "That's everything."
- "And it begins with you."
- A comment engagement question (varied — see AARO_VOICE_REFERENCE.md)

**Comment engagement questions — vary the form:**
- Direct ask: "Are you asking it?"
- Open reflection: "What do you think it's been trying to say?"
- Honest inventory: "What's your honest why?"
- Gentle challenge: "What are you doing right now to actually train your presence?"
- Warm invitation: "Tell me what you notice."
- Cheeky: "Are you doing it? How long?"

## STEP 9 — WHAT TO AVOID (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**Sentence-level:**
- Short declarative fragments stacked: "It's not age. It's not her. It's not testosterone." → WRONG
- Rhetorical questions as transitions: "What most men don't realize?" → WRONG
- "For women who..." / "For men who..." anaphora → Generic and GPT-flat
- Excessive italics instead of CAPS for spoken emphasis
- More than one metaphor per section

**Word-level:**
- "Waistline" (men care about vitality/energy)
- "Genuinely" used as a filler intensifier (use it only when it earns its place)
- "Journey" (too generic)
- "Transform" without specifics (too generic)
- Corporate closer language: "Start your journey," "Take the first step," "That starts with you — and it starts now"

**Conceptual:**
- Making up ideas that aren't grounded in Aaro's actual writing
- Generic coaching concepts that any coach could say
- Diagnosing or naming what the man is struggling with directly
- Ending on a problem or neutral observation
- Forgetting the forward motion — every ending must point somewhere
