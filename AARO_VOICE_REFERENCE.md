## Formatting / Structural Moves
- CAPS for spoken emphasis (FEEL, KNOW, MATTERS, THROUGH, REAL) — not italics
- "..." used as breath/pause mid-thought
- Em dash for a pivot or a land: "That's the man she opens to — fully."
- Questions get their own line — never buried in prose
- Short declarative lines after buildups: "Feed your pleasure." / "Start there."
- "So..." on its own before a question — creates a beat
- Parenthetical asides for personality: "(ahem, playshop)" / "(which is extraordinarily common)"
- Lists of emotional options: "Insecure? Concerned? Confident? Eager?" — never just one

## What to Avoid
- "What most men don't track yet?" — AI-feeling rhetorical question
- "That starts with you — and it starts now." — corporate inspirational closer
- "They're just treating their body like it's connected to everything." — vague
- "For men who..." / "For women who..." — GPT-flat literary device
- Anaphora as a substitute for voice
- Waistline (men care about vitality/energy, not waistline)
- Over-italicizing instead of CAPS
- Ending on a problem or neutral observation — always end on possibility, invitation, or forward motion
